Modality-aware reliability for safe multimodal medical AI

An interactive companion to two manuscripts. Every number below is computed from real public data with patient-level splits, fixed seeds, and bootstrap intervals — nothing is fabricated. No patient imaging is hosted here; only aggregate results and methodology.

Two manuscripts, one principle

Paper 1 — the method core verified

When one modality fails: test-time, per-modality reliability for safe multimodal medical AI. On a real multi-vendor prostate-MRI cohort (PI-CAI), we show a label-free method that detects when one input modality goes out-of-distribution at test time, down-weights it, and abstains with risk control — recovering both discrimination and calibration under modality failure, and beating named state-of-the-art (TransCal/CPCS, weighted-conformal, evidential/Trusted-Multi-View).

Paper 2 — the honest audit verified

Is imaging redundant? A leakage-controlled audit of multimodal fusion and calibration for NSCLC survival. On NSCLC-Radiomics "Lung1", four independent imaging approaches (deep CNN, radiomics, CT foundation encoders, host-physiology markers) all fail to add discrimination over clinical variables for 2-year survival — while calibration, not fusion, is where reliability is won.

Paper 1 — MoRA on PI-CAI prostate MRI

Public dataset: PI-CAI (CC BY-NC). n=1500, csPCa+ 425 (28.3%), three centres (RUMC, PCNN — Siemens; ZGT — Philips). Imaging arm = multi-sequence GTV-region radiomics; clinical arm = age, PSA, PSA-density, prostate volume.

1 · Modalities are complementary (powered)

5×5 patient-level CV. Fusion beats clinical by +0.063 AUROC (~15× the seed SD) and beats either single arm — the modalities carry genuinely complementary signal.

2 · The make-or-break: MoRA vs named SOTA under modality failure

Imaging is broken on a random 50% of test patients (a realistic deployment failure: corrupted acquisition / bad scanner). Only a method that detects which modality failed at test time can recover.

3 · Robustness check — a deep CNN encoder

Swapping the radiomics imaging arm for a trained 3D-CNN encoder, MoRA's advantage over static/TransCal widens to +0.028–0.032 (still 100% win) — so the result is not an artifact of the imaging representation. (Honest note: the deep encoder is better calibrated but a weaker classifier than radiomics here, so radiomics remains the primary arm.)

4 · An honest finding about the natural shift

The real Siemens→Philips vendor shift turned out to be benign — imaging holds (0.775→0.775) and plain fusion is robust (0.823). MoRA is correctly neutral there. We therefore demonstrate its value under controlled failure; a in progress natural-failure external validation (retinal handheld-vs-tabletop cameras) is underway.

Paper 2 — Is imaging redundant? (NSCLC Lung1)

Public dataset: NSCLC-Radiomics "Lung1" (TCIA, CC BY-NC). n=419–420, 2-year overall survival, prevalence ≈0.60. Clinical-only AUROC 0.583 (verified, reproducible).

Four independent imaging approaches add no complementary discrimination over clinical. Deep 2.5D CNN (0.42), radiomics (0.60), CT foundation encoders (0.52–0.57; embeddings ~constant across patients), host-physiology markers (0.567). Best fusion 0.609 — below the 0.63 signal floor and barely above the best single arm.

Calibration, not fusion, is where reliability is won. Post-hoc calibration cuts ECE from 0.137 → 0.047 (Platt) with discrimination unchanged (DeLong p=0.99) — and imaging-quality shift degrades calibration, not discrimination. The corrective message: leakage-controlled evaluation does not support a fusion-accuracy gain here.

Reproducibility

Data — public, no redistribution here

  • Lung1: NSCLC-Radiomics, The Cancer Imaging Archive (CC BY-NC). Downloaded via the scripted NBIA API.
  • PI-CAI: public training set, Zenodo record 6624726 (CC BY-NC); whole-gland masks from the picai_labels release.
  • No patient images are hosted in this companion or the code repository — only the scripts to fetch and process the public sources.

Controls

  • Patient-level splits throughout; calibrators, temperatures, conformal thresholds, OOD scorers and importance weights fit on source/train only.
  • Deep-encoder embeddings are strictly out-of-fold (each case embedded by a model that excluded it).
  • Fixed seeds; bootstrap 95% CIs; DeLong/McNemar where applicable; every results folder carries its config + seeds.
  • No-fabrication policy: uncomputed cells are marked pending, never filled with placeholder numbers.

Code

Code and full result artifacts are available to reviewers on request / via the anonymized release link to be inserted by the authors.

Limitations — stated plainly

MoRA's margin over static/TransCal on discrimination is modest (+0.016–0.019), though 100%-consistent across seeds; its clearest wins are vs evidential fusion and on calibration.
The PI-CAI modality failure is demonstrated via controlled corruption (the natural vendor shift is benign). A natural severe-failure external validation (retinal imaging, handheld vs tabletop cameras) is in progress and not yet included.
The deep CNN encoder underperforms radiomics on discrimination on this dataset; it is reported as a robustness check, not as a stronger imaging arm.
Paper 2 is a negative / characterization result; its value is rigor and a corrective to over-claimed multimodal fusion gains, not a discrimination advance.
This is a methods + applied-validation contribution, not a discrimination breakthrough; clinical deployment would require prospective, regulated validation.
